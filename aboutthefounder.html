About The Founder

Mr. Sreevatsa Subramonian

Mr. Sreevatsa Subramonian graduated from Sri. Jagadguru Renukacharya College of Law (1973-76). Enrolled himself as a lawyer with the Karnataka State Bar Council on 06.08.1976. He started his independent practice a year later on 01.04.1977. He was designated as a Senior Advocate / Counsel by the High Court of Karnataka on 04.03.2008.He is an expert in the field of civil litigation including The Code of Civil Procedure, Arbitration and Conciliation, Family Laws, Property Laws, Banking and Business Laws.

1

integrity

Beyond the courtroom, he is deeply committed to nurturing the next generation of legal minds. As a guest lecturer at esteemed institutions such as Bangalore Law College and St. Joseph's College of Law, he imparts knowledge on vital legal subjects like the Indian Trust Act, Contract Act, Code of Civil Procedure and the Indian Evidence Act has became the first Senior Advocate to be designated from the Mayo Hall Unit of Trial Courts in Bengaluru, underscoring his exceptional abilities in both Trial Courts and Appellate Courts.

2

Action

A formidable presence in the courtroom, Mr. Sreevatsa's reputation as a fierce advocate is well-earned. Yet, beneath his tenacious exterior lies a man of humour, warmth, and unwavering dedication to his clients, colleagues, and the broader community. His enduring legacy continues to inspire and influence the legal landscape in India has earned him widespread acclaim, and his authorship of several seminal books on litigation has solidified his status as a thought leader in the legal field

3

Empathy

His impact extends far beyond his legal practice. A philanthropist with a generous spirit, he has been involved with numerous charitable organisations, including the Lions Club, Round Table, Bangalore Kidney Foundation, and Sumanahalli Leprosy Colony. His dedication to social causes is evident in his unwavering support for children's education in Bengaluru and his close association with the Bettamugilalam Government High School in Krishnagiri District.
